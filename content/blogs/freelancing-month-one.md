Four weeks in. No clients yet. A lot of cold emails. Here's what I learned about going solo in a city that runs on referrals.

## The Chennai reality

Chennai's tech scene is tight-knit. Everyone knows everyone. Job boards exist, but the real opportunities come from "someone who knows someone." As a newcomer to freelancing, that network doesn't build overnight.

## What I tried

1. **Cold outreach** — Sent 50+ emails. Response rate: 4%. Not great, but one of those turned into a real conversation.
2. **Twitter/X** — Shared what I'm building. Got a few DMs. Nothing concrete yet.
3. **Local meetups** — Attended two. Met interesting people. No direct leads, but the vibe was encouraging.

## The mindset shift

I stopped measuring success by "clients landed" and started measuring by "conversations started." One good conversation is worth more than ten ignored emails.

## Next month

More meetups. More building in public. Maybe a small product to show what I can do. We'll see.
